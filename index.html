🇨🇷 San José Museums Guide

Explore Costa Rica's Capital Culture in One City

Museums in San José

  • 1. Museo del Oro Precolombino

    Beneath Plaza de la Cultura

    🌟 Iconic: 1,600+ gold artifacts from pre-Columbian civilizations. Interactive exhibits and a glittering Golden Room.

  • 2. Museo del Jade

    Avenida Central, San José

    💎 Unique: World's largest pre-Columbian jade collection. Don’t miss the rooftop city views.

    🎫 TIP: Wednesday 2x1 tickets for foreigners.

  • 3. Museo Nacional de Costa Rica

    Cuesta de Moras, San José

    🏛️ Historic: Housed in a former military fortress. Exhibits span archaeology, colonial history, and butterfly gardens.

  • 7. Museo de La Salle

    Southwest San José (University campus) Next to the Sabana Park

    🦕 Natural History: Features zoological specimens, mineral collections, and life-size dinosaur replicas. Focus on Central American ecology.

  • 4. Museo de Arte Costarricense

    La Sabana Park

    🎨 Artistic: Costa Rican art from the 19th century onward. Housed in a repurposed airport terminal.

  • 5. Museo de los Niños (Children’s Museum)

    Former Central Penitentiary

    👧👦 Family-Friendly: Interactive science exhibits, a planetarium, and earthquake simulator.

  • 6. Museo de Insectos

    University of Costa Rica

    🐞 Quirky: Over 1 million insect specimens, including glowing beetles and butterflies.

Beyond San José

  • Museo Histórico Cultural Juan Santamaría

    Alajuela

    ⚔️ Focused on Costa Rica’s 1856 national campaign against filibusters.

  • Museo de Guanacaste

    Liberia

    🤠 Celebrates the cowboy culture of Costa Rica’s northwest.

Visiting Tips

  • 📍 Most museums are within walking distance in downtown San José.
  • 🕒 Open Tuesday-Sunday (typically 8:30 AM – 4:30 PM).
  • 🎫 Discounted combo tickets available for Museo del Oro + Museo del Jade.

Poás Volcano

Currently Active
Elevation: 2,708 meters
Last Eruption: January 2025
Distance from SJ: 1.5 hours

Key Features

  • One of the largest active craters in the world
  • Turquoise acidic lagoon (pH < 1)
  • Botts' Dots tactile road system

Visitor Tips

  • Arrive before 10am for best visibility
  • Mandatory gas masks provided
  • UV protection essential
  • 🗺️ Open in Waze

Irazú Volcano

Dormant
Elevation: 3,432 meters (Highest)

Barva Volcano

Wilderness Expedition
Elevation: 2,906m (High-Altitude Camping)
Night Temp: 3-8°C (37-46°F)
Trail Type: Recommended 4x4 and then Hike

Key Features

  • 🌌 Designated wilderness camping zones
  • 🥾 18km network of primal trails
  • ❄️ Year-round cold climate (pack thermals!)

Adventure Essentials

  • ⛺ Campfire permits available at ranger station
  • 🧤 Required gear: 4-season tent, -10°C sleeping bag
  • 🌧️ Waterproof everything - constant mist showers

🫕 Fuel Up at Soda El Maicero in Barva town

Local favorite for:

  • ☕ Pre-hike "Desayuno Montañero" (Mountain Breakfast)
  • 🥘 Post-hike "Olla de Carne" recover stew
  • 🍫 House-made drinks, try the "Resbaladera"
🚗 Get Directions via Waze (5km from trailhead)
